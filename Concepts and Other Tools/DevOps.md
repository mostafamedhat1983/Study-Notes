- **DevOps Definition**: DevOps involves operations and development engineers working together throughout the entire service lifecycle, from design to deployment and production support.
- **Integration of Lean and Agile**: DevOps combines Agile development, Lean product management, and operations.
- **Core Values of DevOps**: The initial values of DevOps are culture, automation, measurement, and sharing (CAMS), with Lean principles added, making it CALMS.
- **Holistic Approach**: Lean principles of flow inform the entire software development process, from product conception to operations, emphasizing continuous improvement and value delivery to the customer.

## CALMS means

- Culture: DevOps starts with collaboration and trust between development, operations, and related teams.
    
- Automation: Teams automate builds, tests, deployments, and other repeatable work to reduce errors and speed delivery.
    
- Lean: The goal is to reduce waste, shorten feedback loops, and improve flow through the software lifecycle.
    
- Measurement: Teams track metrics and outcomes so they can see whether delivery and reliability are improving.
    
- Sharing:  Teams openly share knowledge, documentation, feedback, and lessons learned across development, operations, and other stakeholders; this includes practices like blameless postmortems, where incident learnings are documented and shared to prevent repeat failures

---

## The three ways of DevOps

- Flow (systems thinking): Optimize the whole system so work moves smoothly from development to operations to the customer, instead of optimizing isolated silos.
    
- Feedback (amplifying feedback loops): Shorten and amplify feedback loops so problems are detected early and corrected quickly.
    
- Continual learning and experimentation (continuous improvement): Build a culture where teams experiment, learn from failure, and improve daily work over time.
    

## Simple meaning

In plain words, the Three Ways mean: deliver work faster, detect issues earlier, and keep learning continuously. GitLab and Gene Kim both describe them as maximizing flow, strengthening feedback loops, and encouraging continuous experimentation and learning.

## Good interview answer

You can say: “The Three Ways of DevOps are Flow, Feedback, and Continual Learning. Flow improves end-to-end delivery, Feedback helps catch issues early, and Continual Learning builds a culture of experimentation and improvement.”

## Relation to CALMS

CALMS is a broader DevOps assessment framework, while the Three Ways focus on the underlying principles that drive DevOps behavior and practices. They are related, but they are not the same model.

---
## DevOps Lean & Agile
Lean and Agile are important foundations of DevOps. Agile contributes iterative development and collaboration, Lean contributes flow and waste reduction, and DevOps applies those principles across the full delivery pipeline, including operations.”

## Small distinction

They are related but not identical. Agile, Lean, and DevOps overlap heavily, but each has a different emphasis: Agile centers on development practices, Lean on end-to-end value and efficiency, and DevOps on unifying development and operations for fast, reliable delivery
