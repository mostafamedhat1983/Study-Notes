Conway’s Law says that systems tend to mirror the communication structure of the organizations that build them. In software, that means team boundaries and communication patterns often show up directly in the architecture of the product.

## Simple meaning

If teams are split into isolated silos, the software often becomes split into disconnected parts too. If teams are organized around clear collaboration and ownership, the system is more likely to be modular and easier to evolve.

## DevOps context

Conway’s Law matters a lot in DevOps because DevOps tries to reduce silos between development, operations, and other functions. When teams communicate well and share ownership, delivery and incident response usually become faster and smoother
